# How This Mini-Book Can Help You

Whether you are onboarding onto a new team or just looking to sharpen your day-to-day messaging, this book gathers the communication patterns that tend to make the biggest difference in technical workplaces.

The first two chapters cover the platforms where most conversations happen: Slack and Microsoft Teams. The remaining chapters are platform-agnostic: everyday tech vocabulary, ready-to-use message templates, and a structured approach to incident response communication.

Pick the chapter that matches what you need right now, or read them in order for a full overview.

# Chapters Overview

- [Chapter 1: Platform Essentials: Slack](/pages/communication-tips-chapter-1): Themes, channels, user groups, search, and best practices.
- [Chapter 2: Platform Essentials: Microsoft Teams](/pages/communication-tips-chapter-2): Teams hierarchy, channels, tags, search, meetings, and best practices.
- [Chapter 3: Tech Expressions and Everyday Language](/pages/communication-tips-chapter-3): Common phrases you will hear in stand-ups, code reviews, and team chat.
- [Chapter 4: Message Templates](/pages/communication-tips-chapter-4): Copy-paste templates for meeting absences, time off, and status updates.
- [Chapter 5: Incident Response Communication](/pages/communication-tips-chapter-5): Channel selection, escalation paths, severity definitions, and post-incident checklists.
